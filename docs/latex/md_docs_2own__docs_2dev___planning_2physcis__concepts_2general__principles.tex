In the study of mechanics, particularly when discussing the motion of objects, the terms "{}kinematics"{} and "{}dynamics"{} (or "{}kinetics"{}) refer to different aspects\+:


\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item {\bfseries{Kinematics}}\+: This is the study of motion without considering the causes of the motion. It involves concepts like displacement, velocity, acceleration, and time, without any reference to the force causing the movement. In other words, kinematics is purely descriptive and doesn\textquotesingle{}t delve into the reasons behind the motion.
\item {\bfseries{Dynamics}} (or sometimes referred to as {\bfseries{Kinetics}})\+: This is the study of motion considering the forces and torques that cause it. Dynamics is further divided into\+:
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item {\bfseries{Statics}}\+: Study of forces in equilibrium (i.\+e., systems that are not accelerating).
\item {\bfseries{Kinetics}}\+: Study of forces in non-\/equilibrium (i.\+e., systems that are accelerating).
\end{DoxyItemize}
\end{DoxyEnumerate}

So, in a simplified way\+:


\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item {\bfseries{Kinematics}}\+: How things move.
\item {\bfseries{Dynamics/\+Kinetics}}\+: Why things move.
\end{DoxyItemize}\hypertarget{md_docs_2own__docs_2dev___planning_2physcis__concepts_2general__principles_autotoc_md202}{}\doxysubsection{\texorpdfstring{Physics Concepts To be Investigated}{Physics Concepts To be Investigated}}\label{md_docs_2own__docs_2dev___planning_2physcis__concepts_2general__principles_autotoc_md202}
\hypertarget{md_docs_2own__docs_2dev___planning_2physcis__concepts_2general__principles_autotoc_md203}{}\doxysubsubsection{\texorpdfstring{Statics\+:}{Statics:}}\label{md_docs_2own__docs_2dev___planning_2physcis__concepts_2general__principles_autotoc_md203}

\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item {\bfseries{Equilibrium}}\+: A state where the net force and net moment (torque) on a body are zero, leading to no acceleration.
\item {\bfseries{Free Body Diagram (FBD)}}\+: A graphical representation showing all the external forces acting on a body.
\item {\bfseries{Support Reactions}}\+: Forces exerted by supports or connections. For instance, the reaction force of a table supporting a book.
\item {\bfseries{Moments and Torques}}\+: A force\textquotesingle{}s tendency to rotate an object about an axis.
\item {\bfseries{Trusses}}\+: Structures composed of triangular units used in bridges and roofs. Static analysis helps determine the forces in each member.
\item {\bfseries{Beams}}\+: Structural elements that primarily resist loads applied laterally to the beam\textquotesingle{}s axis. Analysis includes determining shear forces and bending moments.
\item {\bfseries{Centroids and Centers of Gravity}}\+: The point where all the weight of an object appears to act.
\item {\bfseries{Moments of Inertia}}\+: Measures an object\textquotesingle{}s resistance to changes in its rotational motion.
\end{DoxyEnumerate}\hypertarget{md_docs_2own__docs_2dev___planning_2physcis__concepts_2general__principles_autotoc_md204}{}\doxysubsubsection{\texorpdfstring{Kinetics\+:}{Kinetics:}}\label{md_docs_2own__docs_2dev___planning_2physcis__concepts_2general__principles_autotoc_md204}

\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item {\bfseries{Newton\textquotesingle{}s Second Law}}\+: ( F = ma ), relating force, mass, and acceleration.
\item {\bfseries{Linear Motion}} (also known as {\bfseries{Translational Motion}})\+: Movement along a straight line. Concepts related to this include velocity, acceleration, and displacement.
\item {\bfseries{Rotational Motion}}\+: Movement around an axis. Concepts include angular velocity, angular acceleration, and moments of inertia.
\item {\bfseries{Work and Energy}}\+: Concepts related to the work done by forces and the conservation of energy.
\item {\bfseries{Momentum and Impulse}}\+: Concepts involving the product of mass and velocity, and how forces acting over time can change momentum.
\item {\bfseries{Drag Force}}\+: A resistive force acting opposite to the object\textquotesingle{}s relative motion through a fluid (like air or water).
\item Friction\+: The force resisting the relative motion of solid surfaces, fluid layers, or material elements sliding against each other.
\item {\bfseries{Simple Harmonic Motion}}\+: The motion of an object oscillating back and forth, such as a pendulum or a mass on a spring.
\item {\bfseries{Damped and Forced Vibrations}}\+: Situations where external forces or resistive forces (like drag or friction) influence an object\textquotesingle{}s natural vibrations.
